From 9,030 to 52,220: Optimising the Simulation

How adding a realistic 3D printer cycle time exposed a critical production bottleneck — and the step-by-step process of solving it.

The Problem: Adding the 3D Printer

With the basic FESTO line model producing over 238,000 phones/year on a standard shift, I initially had significant spare capacity. However, the brief requires including the 3D printer as the first stage of the process — the source of all the casings feeding into the assembly line.

Understanding the Bottleneck

The bottleneck shifted entirely from the assembly line to the printing stage. A single printer produces one casing every 2 hours — on a standard 8-hour shift over 48 working weeks, that is just 960 casings per printer per year. To reach 50,000 phones/year requires:

Calculation

50,000 phones ÷ 960 casings per printer = 52.1 printers — meaning a minimum of 53 printers running in parallel to theoretically hit the target, before accounting for shift break times.

The Optimisation Process

I ran multiple scenarios, systematically increasing the number of printers until the 50,000 target was met while minimising overproduction and waste. The results of each scenario are documented below.
